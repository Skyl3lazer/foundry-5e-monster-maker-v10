Modifying Values

To change an automated value (hit points, armor class, attack bonuses, etc) for your monster, you can apply a positive or negative modifier.

There are two kinds of modifier you can apply:

  1. Relative: Increase or decrease the automated value by this amount (i.e. av = av +/- x).
  2. Absolute: Replace the automated value entirely with this exact amount (i.e. av = x).
Modifying Abilities

To customise your monster's ability modifiers and saving throws, you can define a series of comma-separated modifiers—for example:

                str=1, int=-2, cha=7

These modifiers can be either relative or absolute in nature.

Ranking Abilities

To automatically calculate your monster's ability modifiers and saving throws, rank their six abilities from the strongest (top) to the weakest (bottom).

Monster Tags

Create tags to define categories and sub-types for your monster—ancestries, power sources, elements, factions, affiliations, etc.

You can create multiple tags by separating each with a comma—for example:

                val1, second value, thirdValue
